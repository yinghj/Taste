Experiments

* The following experiments all measure the acuity of oral sensations at the dorsal tip of the tongue: this region has a high density of taste buds and high sensitivities to taste and tactile stimuli.

Taste Recognition Threshold

Stimulants

A series of taste solutions representing four basic tastes (sweet, salty, sour, and bitter) using sucrose, sodium chloride, tartaric acid, and quinine hydrochloride, respectively.


Independent Variable

Solution concentration level.


Procedure

At the beginning and the end of measurement of each taste quality, participants rinsed out their mouths with distilled water. A filter-paper disk soaked in a given taste solution was placed on the tip of the tongue for 3 seconds. Immediately afterward the experimenter removed the disk and washed the participant's tongue with distilled water using a plastic syringe. Taste solutions were applied in ascending order. The lowest concentration for which the participant reported the existence of stimuli (i.e., compared with distilled water) was taken as the detection threshold, and the lower concentration of 2 consecutive solutions that the participant correctly recognized the taste quality was considered the recognition threshold.

Two-point discrimination threshold

Stimulants

Stimulus probes (2.5 g) that had 2 stimulus points using hard nylon thread with a diameter of 285 μm (Unitika Ltd, Osaka, Japan). The cut end of the nylon thread was flattened by heating and formed a disk-shaped stimulus point with a diameter of 400 μm.


Independent Variable

The interval of 2 stimulus points: ranging from 0.6 mm to 1.8 mm, with 0.2 mm increments.


Procedure

Two points of a probe were simultaneously contacted with the surface of the tip of the participant's tongue for 2 seconds in either the anteroposterior or mediolateral direction, and the participant indicated whether he or she felt the stimulation to be 1 point or 2 points. The space between the probe points was increased until the participant felt 2 points, and the minimum recognition interval was considered the 2-point discrimination threshold. The participants practiced these procedures at the tip of their index finger before actual testing.

Capsaicin threshold

Stimulants

Capsaicin stock solution of 0.3 mmol/L, 91 mg capsaicin (97%; Sigma Chemical, St. Louis, MO): first dissolved in 0.5 ml ethanol and then made up to 100 ml with distilled water.


Independent Variable

Capsaicin Concentration: the stock solution was later diluted with distilled water to yield seven concentration steps.


Procedure

The thresholds of capsaicin were investigated according to the same method used to measure taste threshold. The disk soaked in capsaicin solution was held on the tip of the tongue for 10 seconds. Because capsaicin sensation is known to sensitize and desensitize even at a low concentration, the location of a stimulation disk next to the previous stimulation spot within the tongue tip was changed every time. The lowest concentration at which the participant could feel an irritating sensation before burning pain was regarded as the threshold.
