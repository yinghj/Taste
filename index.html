Question

How does TASTE change over the lifespan?

My tastes are simple: I am easily satisfied with the best. Winston S. Churchill

Background

Sometimes people develop strange eating habits as they age.

"I remember teasing my grandfather because he literally put ketchup or Tabasco sauce on everything," says Hunt, a stay-at-home mom from Austin Texas, "When we would tease him, he would shrug his shoulders and just say he liked it."

Many elderly persons report reduced taste perception of the foods they eat.

Any disturbance of taste and oral somatic sensations can induce this phenomenon. We want to take a look at age-related changes in taste perception and somatic sensations in the tongue to explore potential causes of such decreased taste perception in older persons.

Result

The recognition thresholds of all four basic tastes of elderly participants were significantly higher than those of young participants. On the contrary, age-associated changes were not observed in 2-point discrimination and capsaicin sensation.
Therefore, significant age-associated deterioration was observed in taste but not somatic sensations such as touch and burning pain in the tongue, showing that aging affects taste perception and oral somatic sensations differently. This suggests that decreased taste perception of foods in elderly people may be caused primarily by perceptual loss of taste among oral sensations.
